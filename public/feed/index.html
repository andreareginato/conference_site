The answer is always the same, nobody. To highlight this, it was fun when during a presentation in front of almost 100 people just one hand raised up, clarifying that his domotics system didn't really work. </p> <p> It's true, lots of companies have been working on home automation for many years. Some of them are well-known names like <a href="http://www.crestron.com/">Creston</a>, <a href="http://control4.com">Control4</a> and <a href="http://www.amx.com">AMX</a>, but there is no real market. As there are so few domotics houses, this field looks like a dream for many of us. At this point, the correct question is: <strong>why home automation sucks so much nowadays!?</strong> </p> <p> People tend to think that the cause for the low domotics penetration is price. This is both true and false. Actual domotics systems are quite expensive, but like all technologies, when they start to spread, costs start to get lower and lower. So why, why home automation is not a reality? </p> <p> <strong>The real problem are the existing domotics companies</strong>. They create devices that are not able to speak to each other, trying in this way to keep their market share. The consequences are the impossibility for devices such as lights, appliances, security and alarm systems, gas and smoke sensors, multimedia systems, heating systems to communicate with each other, higher complexities, fewer services, higher prices and more importantly lot of confusion for the final user. </p> <p> Thinking at home automation as companies do today, will not last long. <a href="http://www.lelylan.com/blog/why">Lelylan</a> and new Internet of Things related <a href="http://postscapes.com/companies">Companies</a> will change the way we think at home automation. </p> <blockquote> <p> Let everyone experiment new services, remove complexities, make devices communicate and teach people what home automation really means. </p> <small>Mr. Lelylan</small> </blockquote> <p> If you think home automation sucks, <a href="/collaborations/">join us now</a>. The problem is that people love to complicate things. Thus, there are <a href="http://postscapes.com/internet-of-things-definition">different definitions</a> describing this concept. Conclusion? Just a "little" confusion, making the Internet of Things a concept trying to describe everything, and consequently resulting in nothing. But let's take a step back. What the hell does Internet of Things mean? </p> </br> <blockquote> <strong>A world where physical things are reflected onto the web</strong> </blockquote> </br> <p> This is the final definition we came up with. It apparently does not say too much, right? Well, not really. It completely describes the concept in all its simplicity. So, why this simple concept is so exciting? Because the #IoT power comes out from the combination of the physical objects which can affect the real world, and the web which is the fastest growing technology platform available today. Just two concepts, but two really powerful ones, that combined can definitely change our lives. </p> <p> Think about the web as it is today. Lot of services, mashups, developers and new ideas popping up every day. Now, apply all of this to the Internet of Things and try to imagine all the possible new solutions. Smart meters, smart cities and smart houses are only some of them. </p> <p> Still, I've many doubts. First, I keep reading the word “smart” too many times, and I think there is little smart in the #IoT right now. Second, the #IoT is for sure a nice concept, and I love the idea behind it, but I can't really trust something that can do "everything", just because there is nothing like that. </p> <p> Our personal opinion is that we don't have to start from the #IoT to make it successful. We must start from some of its real applications, building new services on top of it. The one we bet on is the Connected House, but there are lot more out there. Just find the one you love the most and make the Internet of Things real. Nowadays our houses are furnished with numerous devices like appliances, lighting systems, alarms, irrigation systems, smoke and gas detectors, heating, cooling, audio, video systems and much more. There is only a problem, well, a big problem: those devices use different standards and they can't easily communicate with each other. </p> <p> <strong>Lelylan has 3 goals</strong> </p> <blockquote> <ol> <li>Connect your house to the web with a clear and simple set of web services.</li> <li>Enable all devices to communicate with each other.</li> <li>Allow everybody to create new services on top of Lelylan, so that we can speed up innovation in this field.</li> <ol> </blockquote> <p> To make the concept clear, let's think at some of the most important web services available today, like Facebook and Twitter. They offer APIs to access our social graph or our micro blogging data. In the same way, Lelylan wants to define a set of simple, clear and consistent APIs to access all resources interacting with your house through the web. </p> <p> What we aim to do is to map every device to a unique URI which will provide control over it. When such a connection is done, technological limitations disappear and devices become identifiable only through their functionalities. </p> <p> For the first time you have a real chance to change how home automation works.
